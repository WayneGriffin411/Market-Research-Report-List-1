<p><p>The implantable pacemaker market comprises three primary types: traditional pacemakers, implantable cardioverter-defibrillators (ICDs), and biventricular ICDs (bi-V ICDs). Traditional pacemakers regulate heart rhythms by emitting electrical impulses. ICDs monitor heart activity and deliver shocks to restore normal rhythm during life-threatening arrhythmias. Bi-V ICDs enhance cardiac function by providing therapy to both ventricles, benefiting patients with heart failure. Together, these devices play crucial roles in managing arrhythmias and improving patients' quality of life.</p></p>
